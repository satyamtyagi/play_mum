Rules of MuM

Game Elements

  • The game is played with a prime modulus m and exactly 3 heaps.
  • No heap size can ever be divisible by m.

Making a Move

On your turn, choose a heap with size hi > 1 and subtract r from it to replace it with h'i = hi - r, subject to:

  1. Restricted subtraction: 1 ≤ r < m (you can subtract at most m - 1).
  2. No reduction to zero/below: r < hi (heaps cannot be reduced to 0 or below, so h'i ≥ 1).
  3. Divisibility constraint: The resulting size cannot be divisible by m (i.e. h'i ¬≡ 0 (mod m)).

Automatic Consolidation

After a legal move, if all remaining heaps satisfy hi < m, they automatically merge into a single heap of size equal to the product of all heaps:

H = ∏ hi

How to Win

  • The game ends when all heaps are equal to 1.
  • Since a heap of size 1 has no legal moves, the player whose turn it is loses (normal play convention). The last player to move wins!

Winning Invariant (π-strategy)

Calculate the product of all heaps modulo m: Pmod = ∏ hi (mod m).

  • If Pmod ≡ 1 (mod m), the position is losing (P-position). Any move you make will leave a winning position for the opponent.
  • If Pmod ≠ 1 (mod m), the position is winning (N-position). There is always a legal move that forces Pmod ≡ 1 (mod m), putting the opponent in a losing position!
